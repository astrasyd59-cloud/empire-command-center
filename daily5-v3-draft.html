📖 Key Terms
P/E Multiple Compression: When stock price falls even if earnings rise, because investors pay less per dollar of earnings. Common when growth expectations reset. (Graham & Dodd, 1934)
Free Cash Flow (FCF): Cash generated minus capital expenditures. Unlike earnings, can't be fudged with accounting. Warren Buffett's preferred metric since 1986.
200-Day Moving Average: Average price over 200 trading days. Major trend indicator. Price above = bull market, below = bear market. First used by Dow Jones editors in 1900s.

📚 Crypto vs TradFi: How Metrics Differ

Why No P/E for Bitcoin?

Bitcoin has no earnings, no cash flows, no dividends. It cannot be valued using traditional equity models (DCF, P/E, EV/EBITDA). Instead, crypto uses:

  • Stock-to-Flow (S2F): Scarcity model. BTC S2F = 58 (gold = 62). Higher = more scarce.
  • Network Value to Transactions (NVT): Like P/E but for networks. BTC NVT = 35 (fairly valued when 30-50).
  • MVRV Z-Score: Market cap vs realized cap. Currently 2.1 (historical top = 7+).

What IS Similar:

  • Beta: BTC has 1.35x beta vs SPY — moves 35% more than stocks on risk-on/risk-off days.
  • Volume Analysis: Same as stocks. $42B daily volume = liquidity confirmation.
  • Support/Resistance: Technical analysis identical. $90K = psychological support.

📖 Crypto Terms
Stock-to-Flow (S2F): Measures scarcity by comparing existing supply to new production. Created by pseudonymous analyst PlanB in 2019. Bitcoin's S2F doubles every 4 years (halving), theoretically driving price.
MVRV Z-Score: (Market Cap − Realized Cap) / Std Dev. Identifies BTC tops (>7) and bottoms (<0). Realized cap = price at which each BTC last moved. Created by Murad Mahmudov & David Puell in 2018.
