Today's Options Concept: Covered Call Strategy
CFA Level I · Derivatives · Income Generation

What is a Covered Call?

Own 100 shares of stock, sell 1 call option against it. You collect premium immediately, but cap upside above the strike. Best used when you're neutral to slightly bullish — expecting sideways movement.

Applied to AAPL Today

Own AAPL @ $268.54
Sell $275 Call @ $4.50 premium
Yield = $4.50 ÷ $268.54 = 1.68% (30-day)
Annualized ≈ 20%
If AAPL > $275: called away at $275 + $4.50 = $279.50

Key Characteristics (CFA L1 · Derivatives)
Max Profit = Strike Price − Stock Price + Premium Max Profit = $275 − $268.54 + $4.50 = $10.96 (4.1%) Breakeven = Stock Price − Premium Breakeven = $268.54 − $4.50 = $264.04 Downside Protection = Premium Received Only

Covered calls don't protect against major downside — they merely reduce cost basis by the premium amount. If AAPL drops 10%, you still lose ~8.5% after premium. Use when willing to sell shares at the strike price.

Today's Glossary — 4 Terms
📖 Terms to Know
Operating Leverage
The degree to which a company's operating income changes in response to a change in revenue. Companies with high fixed costs (cloud infrastructure, factories) have high operating leverage — small revenue changes create large profit swings. AMZN and NVDA exhibit extreme operating leverage.
Corporate Finance · CFA Level I · Chapter 31
Beta (β)
A measure of a stock's sensitivity to market movements. Beta of 1.0 = moves with the market. Beta > 1.0 = more volatile than market (NVDA at 2.31). Beta < 1.0 = less volatile (defensive). Beta < 0 = inverse correlation (rare). Used in CAPM to calculate expected returns.
Portfolio Management · CFA Level I · Chapter 52
PEG Ratio
Price/Earnings-to-Growth ratio. PEG = P/E ÷ Earnings Growth Rate. Adjusts P/E for growth, enabling comparison between high-growth and mature companies. PEG = 1.0 is often considered fair value. PEG < 1.0 suggests undervaluation; PEG > 1.0 suggests overvaluation.
Equity Valuation · CFA Level I · Chapter 41
Total Value Locked (TVL)
The total value of assets deposited in a blockchain protocol's smart contracts. Ethereum's TVL of $52B represents capital actively used in DeFi lending, staking, and trading. Higher TVL indicates greater network usage and trust. Declining TVL often precedes price weakness.
