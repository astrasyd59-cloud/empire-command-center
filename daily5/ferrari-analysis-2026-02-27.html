Investment Thesis: Bull vs Bear
🐂
Bull Case
  • EV Transition Leadership: Ferrari's first fully electric vehicle (launching 2026) maintains brand exclusivity with limited production, while hybrid models already account for 45% of sales.
  • Purosangue SUV Success: The four-door Purosangue continues to command 18-month waitlists with a $400K+ ASP, expanding the addressable market to family-oriented UHNW buyers.
  • China Recovery Play: China sales declined 18% in 2024 but represent pent-up demand. The new Shanghai flagship and localized marketing should drive 2026 rebound.
  • Pricing Power: Ferrari raised prices 8-12% across models in 2024 without order book impact—a rare demonstration of inelastic demand in automotive.
  • Low Volume Strategy: Intentional supply constraint (13,752 units vs 50K+ for Lamborghini) preserves exclusivity and resale values.
  • F1 Synergy: Dominant 2024 F1 season reinforces brand prestige and justifies technology transfer to road cars.
🐻
Bear Case
  • Luxury Slowdown Risk: Global high-net-worth wealth declined 8% in 2024; prolonged weakness could impact discretionary spending on supercars.
  • EV Transition Costs: €4.4B electrification investment through 2026 pressures margins; EV profitability uncertain until scale achieved.
  • Competition Intensifying: Rimac, McLaren, and Porsche scaling EV supercars; traditional luxury brands (Rolls-Royce, Bentley) expanding into Ferrari's price points.
  • Valuation Premium: Trading at 35.6x P/E vs 18x for luxury peers and 15x for auto sector—requires flawless execution.
  • Regulatory Headwinds: EU ICE ban by 2035 and California emissions rules may force faster transition than planned.
  • Currency Exposure: 70% of costs in EUR but 40% of revenue in USD/Asia FX creates margin pressure on dollar weakness.
