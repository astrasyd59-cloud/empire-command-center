🎯 Options Concept: Straddles & Strangles
7 of 8 — Greek/Concept Rotation

Definition

Straddle: Long ATM call + ATM put (same strike). Strangle: Long OTM call + OTM put (different strikes). Both profit from large moves in either direction.

Interpretation

Long straddles/strangles are volatility plays — you need a large move to overcome premium paid. Max loss = total premium. Used before events when direction is uncertain but movement is expected.

▸ Straddles & Strangles Visualization Key Concept
📚 CFA Level I Formula
Straddle Payoff = |Sₜ - K| - (C + P)\nBreakevens: K ± (C + P)\n\nStrangle Payoff is similar but with two strikes (K₁, K₂).

Key Exam Point: Buy straddles when IV is low before an event. Expect volatility expansion. Risk: The stock doesn't move enough (theta decay). Time is your enemy with long straddles.

Question 1 of 5 · Quantitative Methods
A distribution has negative skewness. What does this indicate?
Correct: B. Negative skewness indicates a long left tail. The mean is pulled toward the tail, making it less than the median.
Question 2 of 5 · Quantitative Methods
For a 95% confidence interval with large sample size, what z-score is used?
Correct: B. For a 95% confidence interval (two-tailed), the z-score is 1.96. This captures 95% of the area under the standard normal curve.
Question 3 of 5 · Quantitative Methods
What does a p-value of 0.03 indicate in hypothesis testing?
Correct: C. The p-value is the probability of observing a test statistic as extreme as, or more extreme than, what was observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true.
Question 4 of 5 · Quantitative Methods
In simple linear regression, R² measures:
Correct: B. R² (coefficient of determination) measures the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variable.
Question 5 of 5 · Quantitative Methods
Heteroskedasticity in regression refers to:
Correct: B. Heteroskedasticity occurs when the variance of error terms is not constant across observations. This violates OLS assumptions and requires robust standard errors.
