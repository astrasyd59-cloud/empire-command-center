Price-to-Earnings Ratio (Chapter 41: Equity Valuation)

P/E = Market Price / Earnings Per Share

Interpretation: Investors pay $33.45 for every $1 of AAPL earnings. This is below the S&P 500 Tech sector average of ~36x, suggesting relative value. However, P/E is only meaningful when comparing firms with similar growth profiles.

ROE = Net Income / Shareholders' Equity

📚 Crypto vs TradFi: Alternative Metrics

Why No P/E for Bitcoin? (Chapter 41: Equity Valuation Limitations)

Bitcoin has no earnings, no cash flows, no dividends. Cannot use DCF, P/E, or EV/EBITDA. Instead, crypto analysts use:

Stock-to-Flow (S2F) = 1 / Inflation Rate = 58 (gold = 62)
NVT Ratio = Market Cap / 24h Volume = 67 (fair = 30-50)
MVRV Z-Score = (Market Cap − Realized Cap) / Std Dev = 1.8 (top = 7+)

CFA Parallel: Like valuing commodities (store of value) or early-stage tech (growth optionality). Requires alternative frameworks outside traditional equity models.

📖 Key Terms
Beta (β): Measures stock volatility relative to market. β = 1.0 = moves with market. β > 1.0 = more volatile. β < 1.0 = less volatile. UNH's 0.41 beta means it moves 41% as much as SPY.
Origin: William Sharpe, 1964 CAPM paper. Greek letter signifies regression slope coefficient.
P/E Multiple Compression: When stock price falls even if earnings rise, because investors pay less per dollar of earnings. Common when growth expectations reset. AAPL's P/E compressed from 38x to 33x.
Origin: Graham & Dodd, "Security Analysis" (1934). Fundamental value investing concept.
Return on Equity (ROE): Net income divided by shareholders' equity. Measures management's effectiveness using invested capital. DuPont analysis breaks ROE into margin × turnover × leverage.
Origin: DuPont Corporation, 1914. Donaldson Brown developed for acquisition analysis.
Risk/Reward Ratio: Compares potential trade profit to potential loss. R/R = (Target − Entry) / (Entry − Stop). Professional traders seek 1:2 or higher. Calculated for all setups above.
Origin: Jesse Livermore, "Reminiscences of a Stock Operator" (1923). Formalized with algorithmic trading rise.
Mean Reversion: Financial theory that prices and returns eventually move back toward average. UNH down 18.7% with unchanged fundamentals suggests potential reversion opportunity.
Origin: Statistical concept formalized by Charles Dow (1880s). Applied to markets by Bogle & Graham.
