Normalized P/E for Cyclical Stocks (CFA L1 · Equity Valuation)
Spot P/E = $147.28 ÷ $6.70 = 21.98x ← misleading in cycle peak Normalized Earnings = Average EPS through full commodity cycle Mid-cycle EPS ≈ $8.50 (using 5-yr average) Normalized P/E = $147.28 ÷ $8.50 = 17.3x ← more reliable Rule: Never use spot P/E for energy, mining, or commodity companies.

Today's Options Concept: Bull Call Spread
CFA Level I · Derivatives · Topic 8 of 10

What is a Bull Call Spread?

Buy a call at a lower strike (K₁), sell a call at a higher strike (K₂). Same expiry. You pay a net debit. Profit is capped. Risk is also capped. Used when you're moderately bullish — not expecting a moonshot.

Applied to AAPL Today

Buy AAPL $260 Call @ $8.50 premium
Sell AAPL $275 Call @ $3.20 premium
Net Debit = $5.30 (max loss)
Max Profit = ($275 − $260) − $5.30 = $9.70
R/R ≈ 1.83:1 · Breakeven = $265.30

Payoff Diagram at Expiry — Bull Call Spread (AAPL $260/$275)
Key Greeks to Know (CFA L1 · Derivatives)
Delta (Δ) — How much option price moves per $1 in stock Long $260 Call: Δ ≈ 0.62 | Short $275 Call: Δ ≈ 0.35 Net Δ ≈ 0.27 (spread moves $0.27 per $1 in AAPL) Theta (Θ) — Time decay per day. Enemy of option buyers. Buy low Theta positions when IV is low. Vega (ν) — Sensitivity to implied volatility changes. Spreads have lower net Vega → less IV risk than naked calls. Max Profit = Strike Spread − Net Premium = $15 − $5.30 = $9.70 Max Loss = Net Premium Paid = $5.30 Breakeven = Lower Strike + Net Premium = $260 + $5.30 = $265.30
⚡ Fun Fact
The Black-Scholes model (1973) that revolutionized options pricing was considered so important that Myron Scholes and Robert Merton won the 1997 Nobel Prize in Economics for it. Fischer Black had already passed away. The irony? Their hedge fund LTCM still blew up in 1998, proving even Nobel Prize winners can be wiped out by ignoring tail risk.

Today's Glossary — 4 Terms
📖 Terms to Know
Net Interest Margin (NIM)
The difference between what a bank earns on loans and what it pays on deposits, expressed as a percentage of interest-earning assets. When rates rise, NIM typically expands — which is why bank stocks rally in rate hiking cycles. JPM's NIM expansion is the core bull thesis.
Fixed Income & Banking · CFA Level I · Chapter 36
Implied Volatility (IV)
The market's forward-looking estimate of how much an asset will move, derived from current option prices using Black-Scholes. High IV = expensive options. The VIX is essentially the IV of the S&P 500 — a real-time fear gauge. IV tends to spike during market selloffs and collapse during calm periods.
Derivatives · CFA Level I · Chapter 57
Medical Loss Ratio (MLR)
The percentage of health insurance premiums spent on actual medical claims. UNH's MLR spiked to 85.5% — meaning $85.50 of every $100 premium went to claims. The ACA requires MLR ≥ 80%. Rising MLR is the core bear case for managed care stocks right now.
Healthcare Equity Analysis · Industry-Specific Metric
Stock-to-Flow (S2F)
A scarcity model used for Bitcoin and commodities. S2F = Existing Supply ÷ New Annual Production. Gold has S2F ≈ 62 (takes 62 years of mining to double supply). Post-halving Bitcoin has S2F ≈ 120 — twice as scarce as gold by this metric. Critiqued as overly simplistic but widely cited in crypto circles.
