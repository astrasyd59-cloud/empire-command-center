Understanding Delta
Options Greeks · Concept 1 of 8

What is Delta?

Delta (Δ) measures how much an option's price changes for a $1 move in the underlying stock. Calls have positive delta (0 to 1), puts have negative delta (-1 to 0).

Probability Proxy

Delta approximates the probability an option expires in-the-money. A 0.30 delta call has ~30% chance of finishing ITM.

Δ = Change in Option Price / Change in Underlying Price Example: AAPL call with Δ = 0.60 If AAPL rises $1 → Call gains ~$0.60
Delta Behavior by Moneyness Long Call
CFA Exam Tip
Delta hedging: To neutralize directional exposure, hold (1/Δ) options per share. A 0.50 delta requires 2 calls per 100 shares for delta-neutral position.

Glossary
Today's Terms
Beta (β)
A measure of a stock's volatility in relation to the overall market. A beta of 1 means the stock moves with the market. Less than 1 means less volatile; greater than 1 means more volatile.
Origin: William Sharpe, 1964, Capital Asset Pricing Model
Carry Trade
A strategy involving borrowing in a low-interest-rate currency and investing in a higher-yielding currency, profiting from the interest rate differential.
Popularized in forex markets during Japan's low-rate era (1990s-2000s)
Economic Moat
A sustainable competitive advantage that protects a company's market share and profitability from competitors, coined by Warren Buffett.
Coined by Warren Buffett, inspired by medieval castle defenses
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings ratio compares a company's share price to its earnings per share. A valuation metric showing how much investors pay per dollar of earnings.
Benjamin Graham, "The Intelligent Investor" (1949)
