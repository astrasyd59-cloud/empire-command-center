🎯 Options Concept: Put-Call Parity
6 of 8 — Greek/Concept Rotation

Definition

Put-call parity defines the relationship between put and call prices with the same strike and expiration. Arbitrage exists if this relationship is violated.

Interpretation

Synthetic positions can be created: Stock + Put = Call + Bond (risk-free). This relationship keeps option prices aligned and prevents risk-free profits.

▸ Put-Call Parity Visualization Key Concept
📚 CFA Level I Formula
C + PV(K) = P + S\nWhere: C=Call price, P=Put price, S=Stock price, K=Strike, PV=present value\n\nRearranged: C - P = S - PV(K)

Key Exam Point: Put-call parity applies only to European options on non-dividend stocks. Violations imply arbitrage opportunities that traders quickly eliminate.

Why This Topic Matters
Ethics (15-20% of exam) forms the foundation of the CFA program. Standards govern professional conduct and are heavily tested with scenario-based questions.

Fiduciary Duty

Act in client's best interest, place client interests above your own

Material Nonpublic Information

Cannot trade on MNPI; mosaic theory allows combining public info

📚 Key Formulas — Ethical and Professional Standards
Standard I(A): Know and comply with laws Standard I(B): Maintain independence and objectivity Standard III(A): Loyalty, prudence, and care to clients Standard V(A): Diligence and reasonable basis for research

Worked Example: A portfolio manager receives MNPI about a merger. Standard II(A) prohibits trading. They must not act on the information and should make reasonable efforts to achieve public dissemination.

Under Standard I(A) Knowledge of the Law, a member discovers their employer is violating securities regulations. What should they do first?
Correct: B. Standard I(A) requires members to first attempt to have the firm cease the violation. If unsuccessful, they may need to dissociate and potentially report to regulators.
Question 2 of 5 · Ethical and Professional Standards
A CFA charterholder includes their CFA designation in large font on their business card. This is:
Correct: B. Standard VII(B) prohibits exaggerating the meaning or implications of having the CFA designation. The designation should not be larger than the charterholder's name.
Question 3 of 5 · Ethical and Professional Standards
Which action most likely violates Standard II(A) Material Nonpublic Information?
Correct: B. Information overheard in an elevator is material nonpublic information. The Mosaic Theory allows combining public information, but trading on MNPI is prohibited.
Question 4 of 5 · Ethical and Professional Standards
Under Standard III(A) Loyalty, Prudence, and Care, to whom do members owe their primary duty?
Correct: C. Members owe their primary duty to clients — the beneficial owners of the assets being managed. This duty includes acting with care and exercising prudent judgment.
Question 5 of 5 · Ethical and Professional Standards
A portfolio manager allocates oversubscribed IPO shares to fee-paying clients only. This:
Correct: B. Standard III(B) requires fair dealing with all clients. IPO allocations must be made on a pro-rata or other fair basis, not based on fee arrangements.
Today's Glossary — 4 Terms · Ethical and Professional Standards
📖 Terms to Know
Fiduciary Duty
The duty to act in the best interest of the client, placing client interests above one's own. Requires loyalty, care, and acting within the scope of authority.
CFA Level I · Standard III: Duties to Clients
Material Nonpublic Information
Information that could affect the price of a security and is not available to the general public. Trading on such information violates Standard II(A).
CFA Level I · Standard II: Integrity of Capital Markets
Soft Dollars
Commission dollars used to purchase research and other services that benefit the client. Must benefit the client, not the manager.
CFA Level I · Standard III(A): Soft Dollar Standards
Independence and Objectivity
Members must maintain independence and objectivity in their professional activities, free from conflicts of interest or bias.
