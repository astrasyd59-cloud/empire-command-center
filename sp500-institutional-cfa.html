Defensive vs. Cyclical Sectors:
Defensive sectors (Consumer Staples, Utilities, Health Care) tend to outperform during economic downturns as demand remains stable. Cyclical sectors (Consumer Discretionary, Financials, Industrials) outperform during expansion phases. Technology exhibits both growth and defensive characteristics in modern markets.
CFA Level I Industry Life Cycle Analysis

Industries progress through stages: Embryonic → Growth → Shakeout → Mature → Decline

  • Technology: Late Growth / Early Shakeout (consolidation around AI leaders)
  • Health Care: Mature (GLP-1 drugs creating new growth cycle)
  • Energy: Decline / Reinvention (transition to renewables)
  • Financials: Mature (fintech disruption)

CFA Level I Reading 43: Market Efficiency

Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) forms the theoretical foundation for understanding market behavior:

  • Weak-form efficiency: Technical analysis cannot consistently produce excess returns
  • Semi-strong efficiency: Fundamental analysis of public information cannot produce excess returns
  • Strong-form efficiency: Even private information is reflected in prices

Current market dynamics suggest semi-strong efficiency with pockets of behavioral inefficiency (meme stocks, AI narrative momentum).
