How to start trading options with limited capital while staying alive long enough to learn

📖 Step A: How to Read an Options Chain

1

Expiry Date (DTE)

DTE = Days to Expiry. Shorter DTE = faster time decay (good for sellers), but less time to be right. For beginners: 20-45 DTE is the sweet spot. Avoid 0-7 DTE ("0 DTE") until you have 100+ trades under your belt.

2

Strike Prices (ITM, ATM, OTM)

ATM (At-The-Money): Strike ≈ Current price. Highest premium, 50/50 chance.
OTM (Out-of-The-Money): Strike below current price (for puts). Lower premium, higher probability of profit.
ITM (In-The-Money): Strike above current price. High premium, behaves like stock.

3

Bid/Ask Spread (Liquidity)

Always check the spread. Bid = what buyers will pay. Ask = what sellers want. Wide spreads (>10% of premium) = poor liquidity. You'll lose money just entering/exiting. Look for tight spreads (<5%).

4

Implied Volatility (IV)

IV Rank (0-100%): Where current IV sits vs. 52-week range.
• 0-30%: Low IV (bad for selling, good for buying)
• 30-70%: Medium IV (neutral)
• 70-100%: High IV (good for selling, fear premium)

Rule: Sell options when IV Rank > 50%.

5

Delta (Probability Proxy)

Delta ≈ Probability of expiring ITM. A put with 0.20 delta has ~20% chance of being ITM at expiry, or 80% chance of expiring worthless (good for sellers). Target: 0.15-0.30 delta for OTM puts.

🎯 Strategies for 1,000 AUD Capital

✅ PUT CREDIT SPREADS (Recommended)

Defined Risk Beginner-Friendly

Sell a put at one strike, buy a cheaper put further down. Max loss = width between strikes minus premium received. Max profit = premium collected.

Example: DAX Mar 20 Strike Premium
Sell Put 24,800 +€95
Buy Put 24,400 -€35
Net Credit — €60 (~$100 AUD)
Max Risk 400 points × €5 €2,000 (~$540 AUD)
Position Sizing for 1,000 AUD

Maximum 1 contract. This spread uses $540 of your $1,000, leaving $460 for other trades or cushion. Never risk more than 50% of capital on one trade.

⚠️ IRON CONDORS (Advanced)

Higher Complexity Defined Risk

Sell a put spread AND a call spread. Profits if price stays in a range. Higher probability, lower max profit. Requires understanding of both sides. Save for after you've mastered put spreads.

❌ WHY NOT 0 DTE?

High Risk Avoid as Beginner

0 DTE (same-day expiry) offers huge theta decay but:
• Gamma risk is extreme (price moves kill you fast)
• No time to adjust if wrong
• Requires constant monitoring
• One bad trade wipes out 10 good ones

Your friend does 0 DTE. He has experience and larger capital. You don't. Yet.

🎓 CFA Thought Nuggets

1. Risk-Adjusted Returns

Making $100 on $500 risk (20% return) sounds great. But if you have an 80% chance of success, your expected value is: (0.80 × $100) + (0.20 × -$500) = $80 - $100 = -$20. You're expected to lose money despite the high win rate!

Expected Value Formula:

EV = (Probability of Profit × Max Profit) + (Probability of Loss × Max Loss)

For positive edge: (POP × Max Profit) > ((1 - POP) × |Max Loss|)

2. Position Sizing (Kelly Criterion Simplified)

The Kelly Criterion tells you optimal bet size: f = (bp - q) / b
Where: b = odds, p = win probability, q = loss probability

With 1,000 AUD and a trade risking $540: Kelly suggests 2-5% of portfolio. You'd need $10,000-25,000 to safely take this trade. With 1,000 AUD, you're essentially gambling.

3. Why Retail Traders Fail at Options

  • Overconfidence: Win 5 trades, think you're a genius, go big on #6, blow up.
  • Position Sizing: Risking 50%+ of capital on single trades.
  • Early Exit: Taking profits too early, letting losers run (disposition effect).
  • Complexity Creep: Adding legs to trades they don't understand.
  • Gamma Blindness: Not respecting how fast short-dated options move.

🚀 Your 4-Week Action Plan

1

Week 1-2: Paper Trade

Open a paper trading account (CMC, Interactive Brokers). Trade 10 put credit spreads on IWM or SPY. Track every trade: entry, exit, P&L, what you learned. Do not use real money yet.

2

Week 3: First Live Trade

Fund account with $500 (not the full 1,000). Trade 1 contract maximum. Set a hard stop: if you lose $100, stop trading for the week and review.

3

Week 4: Scale or Stop

If profitable after 5+ live trades with proper sizing: continue with remaining $500. If unprofitable: go back to paper trading for another 2 weeks.

4

Ongoing: The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your portfolio on a single trade. With $1,000, that's $10. This seems tiny, but it's how you survive long enough to learn. Scale up position size only as your account grows.

📝 Bottom Line

The Honest Truth

With 1,000 AUD, you're not going to get rich trading options. You're going to learn. Treat this as tuition money, not investment capital. Your goal: survive 100 trades while learning the mechanics. Profit is a bonus.

Start with: IWM put credit spreads, 30-45 DTE, 0.15-0.20 delta, 1 contract max.
