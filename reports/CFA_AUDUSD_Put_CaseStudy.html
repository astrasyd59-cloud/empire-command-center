📚 CFA Lens: Options Fundamentals

1. Put Option Basics (CFA Level I — Derivatives)

A put option gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell the underlying asset at a predetermined price (the strike).

Long Put Payoff at Expiry = max(0, Strike − Spot Price) − Premium

If Spot > Strike: Option expires worthless (loss = premium paid)
If Spot < Strike: Profit = (Strike − Spot) − Premium

2. Moneyness (CFA Level I — Option Markets)

Moneyness Classification:

• ITM (In-the-money): Spot < Strike for Puts (would profit if exercised now)
• ATM (At-the-money): Spot ≈ Strike
• OTM (Out-of-the-money): Spot > Strike for Puts (no intrinsic value)

This trade is OTM: Spot (0.7076) > Strike (0.6900). The entire $93 premium is time value — no intrinsic value yet.

📖 Key Terms for CFA Exam
Put Option: A contract giving the holder the right (not obligation) to sell an asset at a set price (strike) before expiry. Used for bearish bets or hedging long positions.
CFA Level I: Derivatives & Risk Management
Intrinsic Value: For puts: max(0, Strike − Spot). The immediate profit if exercised. OTM options have zero intrinsic value.
CFA Level I: Option Valuation
Time Value (Extrinsic Value): Option premium minus intrinsic value. Reflects probability of becoming ITM before expiry. Decays to zero at expiration.
CFA Level I: Option Pricing
Theta: Rate of time value decay per day. Always negative for long options. "Time is the enemy of the option buyer."
CFA Level II: Greeks & Risk Measures
Leverage in Options: Controlling large notional exposure with small premium. Amplifies both gains and losses. This trade controls $10,000 notional with only $93.
