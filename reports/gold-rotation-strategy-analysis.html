🚨 THE CORRELATION ASSUMPTION: MAKE OR BREAK

The entire strategy hinges on this assumption: Gold rises when equities fall during crisis periods.

Historical equity-gold correlation:

  • Normal times: ~0 (no correlation)
  • Crisis periods: -0.3 to -0.5 (negative, risk-off bid)
  • Liquidity crises: +0.5 to +0.8 (everything sold together)

The danger: In March 2020, gold fell 12% alongside equities as investors sold everything to meet margin calls. The strategy would have been long gold into a crash. It recovered, but this is the correlation-breakdown risk.

AQR Research: "Gold's diversification benefit is conditional on the nature of the crisis. Sovereign debt/currency crises: gold works. Liquidity crises: gold fails."

📅 REGIME DEPENDENCY: THE 1980s-2000 PROBLEM

The backtest starts in 2007 — coincidentally the start of a favorable regime for gold (rising from $700 to $2,070 peak). But what about the prior 20 years?

1980-2000 Gold Bear Market:

  • Gold fell from $850 (1980) to $255 (1999): -70%
  • Equities (S&P 500) gained 1,000%+ over same period
  • Strategy would have rotated into a falling asset while missing the equity boom
  • SMA crossovers generate repeated false signals in choppy markets

Ray Dalio's warning: "This strategy requires the right macro regime. It's not evergreen."
