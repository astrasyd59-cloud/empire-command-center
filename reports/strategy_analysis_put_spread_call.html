Put Spread + Long Call

Zero-Cost Convexity Strategy · Niall's Play
The Strategy Breakdown
What Niall Is Proposing

This is a "Risk Reversal" or "Zero-Cost Collar" variant that uses a put spread instead of a single short put. The genius: you collect premium from the put spread, use it to buy a call, and end up with near-zero net cost and zero net theta.

Key Insights
Why This Strategy Works
  1. Convexity for Free: You get optionality (asymmetric upside) without paying time decay. The put spread theta roughly cancels the long call theta.
  2. Defined Risk: Unlike naked puts or synthetic longs, your max loss is capped at the width of the put spread (1,000 points).
  3. No Time Pressure: Niall's right — use long-dated expiries. Without theta drag, you can hold through volatility and wait for your catalyst.
  4. Leverage Without Margin: You're controlling upside exposure with minimal capital outlay. The "cost" is the risk between 20k-21k.
  5. Better Than Stock: For a "punt" on a big move, this beats buying shares. Same upside exposure, limited downside, no capital tie-up.

"The best trades are ones where you're paid to take risk, not pay to take risk. This structure flips the script — you're essentially renting convexity for free by accepting defined downside risk."

Best US Candidates for This Strategy

High-beta tech names with earnings catalysts: NVDA, TSLA, PLTR, COIN. Look for:

Bottom Line

Niall's strategy is legit. It's a sophisticated way to get long exposure with defined risk and zero time decay cost. The trade-off: you cap your downside protection (only protected below 20k) and you miss the 20k-22k range (small gains vs stock).
